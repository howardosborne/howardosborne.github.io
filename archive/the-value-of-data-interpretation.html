The value of data gathering and the greater value of interpreting it

We have never had so much data and thanks to Google, that data has never been so accessible.

Google Ngram gives us the opportunity to scan word and phrase data from books and look for the meaning buried within. Surely this is proof that we live in the information age? Apparently not...

Whilst there is great value in gathering data, there is more value in interpreting it. Take these examples:

Proof that aliens have got bored with us - a search for 'ufo'

or is it proof that we have got better at English grammar - until they got bored with us...

Results for 'UFO' (in upper case)

Proof of lack of originality - spot the blip around 1980 for the original Harry Potter

...until we look at the source of the 1980 data

A short history of the English speaking world

Proof that seatbelts save lives?

do seatbelts actually prevent car crashes or deaths resulting from them?

proof that everybody has their day, - O'Hara, Osama, Obama

can't see the graph? - there's a bug in Google's code for escaping single quotes so we can't see Maureen O'Hara - we'll use a physicist instead

proof that everybody has their day - Oppenheimer, Osama, Obama
